CSS Specificity

  • Inline styles (e.g. style="color: red;") have the highest specificity, with a value of 1000.
  • ID selectors (e.g. #example) have a specificity of 100.
  • Attribute selectors with classes (e.g. [class="example"]) have a specificity of 10.
  • Class selectors (e.g. .example) have a specificity o
  • Type selectors (e.g. div, p, h1, etc.) have a specificity of 1
  • Pseudo-elements (e.g. ::before, ::after, etc.) have a specificity of 1
  • Attribute selectors (e.g. [attr], [attr=value], etc.) have a specificity of
  • Pseudo-classes (e.g. :hover, :active, etc.) have a specificity o
  • Combinators (e.g. +, ~, >, and whitespace) have a specificity of 0
  • Universal selectors (e.g. *) and negation pseudo-class (e.g. :not()) have a specificity of 0.
